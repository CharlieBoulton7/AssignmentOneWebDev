Front End Developer

The link above takes you to the job description page, adertised on TotalJobs. This job is an application for a Front End Developer role looking at creating interactive, eye catching webpages for various clients. Permanent position paying £25k – 30k.

C# Web Developer

C# Web Developer

The link above takes you to the job description page, adertised on TotalJobs. This job is around developing software applications that would be used for a local Northampton based company. Permanent position paying an annual salary of between £25k – 40k.

Similarities

- Both jobs include working in teams to develop the projects
- Annual salary roughly the same.
- Weekly Hours.
- Working on various projects.
- Both require experience in HTML and CSS.

Differences

- Job number one requires more experience across different OS’s. As well as needing experience in HTML and CSS, applicant would also be expected to have knowledge of C#, ASP.NET. SQL Server, JQuery. If the applicant also has experience in Visual Code this is seen as an advantage.
- Job number two sees experience in UI and UX as an advantage.
- Major difference is job description. As said, job number one is more around developing software applications, mainly to be used for own company. Job number two evolves developing web pages and interfaces for various clients.
- Job number two talks about needing to demonstrate competence with software, ideally being done through a portfolio of work the applicant may have worked on.
- Job number one if advertised through a recruitment agency and job number one is advertised by employee.

Overall Comments

Looking at the two jobs initially shows that that there are relative similarities between the two. Both jobs come across as opportunities for applicants who are maybe towards the beginning of their career, somewhere for them to start out. With similar salaries and workloads within teams, they seem ideal for people looking to make a step up from junior job roles or similar levels of experience. In terms of how the two jobs present themselves through applications, It seems like the Front End developer role is more to the point, informing the applicant of what would be expected doing the interview process and onwards. Comparing this to the Web Developer role shows that the information provided is more detailed, giving the reader more general information such as benefits, information on the company and a clear view of required experience.
